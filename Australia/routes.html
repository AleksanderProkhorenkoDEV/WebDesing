CULTURAL

Explore the rich tapestry of Australia's cultural and natural wonders as you embark on these captivating journeys. Traverse ancient caves adorned with mesmerizing rock carvings that tell stories of times long past.

Immerse yourself in the vibrant tapestry of native wildlife, their habitats echoing with the melodies of Australia's unique ecosystems.

Delve into the centuries-old tradition of oyster hunting, a skill passed down through generations.

Join our immersive guided First Nations walks, where knowledgeable guides will unveil the secrets of the land, sharing stories that connect the present with the ancient roots of Australia's indigenous heritage.

Experience the profound beauty and cultural significance of the Australian landscape through these enriching adventures

Day 1: EXPLORE THE WORLD’S OLDEST LIVING RAINFOREST

The Daintree Rainforest, about two hours north of Cairns, is – in a word–breathtaking.
br This World Heritage-listed site is home to the Kuku Yalanji people, known as the true rainforest people.

Kuku Yalanji people, the rainforest is more than trees and vines; contained within its canopy are traditional medicines, delicious native foods and sacred stories.

Day 2: DISCOVER ANCIENT WORKS OF ART

Aboriginal rock art is one of the oldest art forms in the world, so witnessing these ancient depictions is like looking directly into another.

Some of Australia's most impressive indigenous artwork can be found in South Australia's Ikara-Flinders Ranges National Park, a mecca for Aboriginal people.

Cultural sites such as the Sacred Canyon, home of the Adnyamathanha "rock" people.

Day 3: FORAGE FOR OYSTERS

The traditional custodians of the Dampier Peninsula, the Bardi (land) and Jawi (island) people, have been working with their natural environment for dozens thousands of years old.

Using sustainable hunting techniques, fishing practices and bush tucker for food and medicinal purposes.

Here, about two hours north of Broome, you can combine a cultural experience with world-class seafood.

Day 4: EMBARK ON A LIFE-CHANGING JOURNEY

Lutruwita, Tasmania, is steeped in First Nations culture and one of the best ways to experience it is by traversing the land with a guide aboriginal wukalina Walk is more than a walk; It is a spiritual, cultural and physical journey time

In this experience, you will be invited to "takara waranta" (walk with us) around your guides' homeland in eastern Tasmania, including notable sites such as the Bay of Fires and National Park Mount William.

Day 5: WANDER THROUGH ANCIENT CAVES

Are you looking for an experience that you can't find anywhere else in the world?

Among Australia's fascinating ancient landscapes is the phenomenal Cutta Cutta Caves National Park, just 30 minutes south of Katherine in the Northern Territory.

Like many things in this country, These tropical cave systems, formed millions of years ago, are unique to Australia, as is the variety of wildlife that inhabits them..

NATURAL

When the weather warms in Australia, so does its wildlife. The warm summer months of December, January and February are a fantastic time to

Join a wildlife viewing tour to see some of the country's most iconic creatures, especially as many of them have babies At this time of year.

Day 1: WALK THE RED CRAB CARPET

You won't see anything like this anywhere else. Every year, when the first summer rains begin to fall, 50 million red crabs march en masse from the lush rainforest in the middle of Christmas Island (a 3.5-hour flight from Perth) to the sea, where they mate and spawn.

The party in the largest beach in the world. It's an extraordinary sight, with roads and paths along the island transformed into billowing red ribbons of crabs that cover all the obstacles in their path

Day 2: JOIN A KILLER WHALE FEAST

Witnessing the largest congregation of orcas in a feeding frenzy is one of nature's most exciting spectacles, and the best place to experience in Australia is the Bremer Canyon, an underwater canyon that is twice as deep as the Grand Canyon in the United States.

It is in front of the Bremer Bay shoreline, a two-hour drive east of Albany on the state's southern coast. Here, every summer, hundreds of orcas and other predators such as squid giants, sperm whales and great white sharks gather to hunt and feast.

Day 3: WITNESS HATCHING TURTLES

Watching a tiny turtle emerge from an egg and scamper into the sea is a life-affirming experience. Mon Repos Turtle Centre, near Bundaberg (a 4.5-hour drive north of Brisbane), is home to the largest number of nesting loggerhead turtles on Australia’s east coast

Day 4: JOIN A KILLER WHALE FEAST

A two-hour drive southeast of Phillip Island, Phillip Island is famous for its nightly penguin parade, but not many people know that nearby Seal Rocks is also home to 6,000 or so seals, the largest colony of Australian fur seals in the country

The best way to see them is by boat because the seals, who are very curious, come quite close to the drifting vessel for a closer look

You can see them all year, but they breed during the summer, so you’ll see lots of very cute seal pups frolicking in the water and scampering across the rocks.

Day 5: WALK WITH BABY WOMBATS

If there's anything cuter than a baby wombat – called a joey, just like a baby kangaroo – then we'd like to see it.

There are so many animals and birds on tiny Maria Island, east of Hobart, that it’s nicknamed “Noah’s Ark”

Think: Tasmanian devils, wallabies by the hundreds and plenty of wombats.

In fact, you won’t be on the island for very long before you encounter one of these pudgy little critters – they weigh around 35 kilograms (77 pounds).

AVENTURE

Australia promises an exhilarating adventure that will undoubtedly set your heart racing.

Here, you have the opportunity to abseil down the world's highest dam, providing an adrenaline-pumping descent against a breathtaking backdrop.

Additionally, navigate through ancient rainforests via river rafting, immersing yourself in the lush surroundings and the thrill of the rushing waters.

For the daring at heart, Australia offers the extraordinary chance to dive with great white sharks, an experience that takes you face-to-face with one of the ocean's most majestic predators.

Embark on these heart-pounding escapades and discover the thrilling side of Australia's natural wonders. Tackling these daring activities will not only push your limits but also create unforgettable memories in the Land Down Under.

Day 1: ISLAND HOPPING ON THE WHITSUNDAYS

The Whitsunday Islands in Queensland offer some of the best sailing in the world, thanks to perfect winds, calm seas and 74 islands to explore (69 of which are uninhabited).

Day 2: LUXURY TRAIN ACROSS AUSTRALIA

Known as one of the world's greatest train journeys, a trip on the Ghan will take you cross-country from Darwin to Adelaide (or vice versa).

As you travel through the tropical greens of the Top End, the desert sands of the Red Center and the rugged mountains of the Flinders Ranges, you will see some of the most diverse and beautiful landscapes in Australia.

Day 3: SUNBATHE WITH KANGAROOS AT LUCKY BAY

It doesn’t get more iconically Australian than lying on a white sandy beach beside a kangaroo.

Resident roos are known to regularly sun themselves at a handful of Aussie beaches, the most famous of which is Lucky Bay in Western Australia’s Esperance.

This stretch of white sand and turquoise water is an idyllic place to catch some rays with a roo or two.

Be sure to pack your camera to capture this iconic moment from a friendly distance

Day 4: CRUISE THE KIMBERLEY

One of the best ways to see the Kimberley (one of the last true wilderness areas on Earth) is by cruise ship.

Two billion years of natural history are on show along the isolated Kimberley coast in Western Australia, and an expedition cruise lets you get up close.

Marvel at the region’s spectacular rocky shores, ochre-coloured gorges, lush waterfalls and complex river systems, all while enjoying gourmet meals and luxurious sleeping quarters.

Day 5: SWIM WITH UNIQUE MARINE LIFE

Swimming with Australia's marine life is the stuff of bucket lists. Luckily, there are experienced tour guides ready to make your dreams come true

Glide alongside humpback whales, come mask-to-fin with a whale shark, splash around with adorable sea lions and swim with a playful pod of dolphins.

FAMILIAR

Embark on an enriching journey with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander travel experiences, tailored to captivate families. These experiences offer a fantastic avenue for both young and old to delve into the rich cultural tapestry of Australia's indigenous peoples.

Discover ancient traditions, vibrant art forms, and engaging stories that provide an educational and memorable experience for every family member.

These travel encounters create a wonderful opportunity for kids of all ages to gain insights into an ancient culture that continues to thrive in the present day. Immerse yourselves in the living history of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities, fostering a deeper understanding of their traditions, values, and connection to the land.

With interactive activities and engaging narratives, these experiences offer a unique and enlightening adventure for families seeking to broaden their cultural horizons.

Day 1: THE GRAMPIANS

The sandstone ridges of the Grampians, three hours west of Melbourne, offer a family adventure alongside a sophisticated food and wine scene.

Kangaroos and emus roam the tranquil bushland, while adrenaline-inducing activities will turn the mountain range into your personal playground.

Test your strength on a rock climbing session, immerse yourself in Aboriginal culture in Brambuk and paddle canoes on Bellfield Lake

Day 2: SYDNEY

With beautiful coastal walks, Aboriginal tours and wildlife experiences available right in the heart of the city, Sydney is the perfect spot to balance coastal adventures with cultural immersion. Kids and teens alike will love surfing, snorkelling and soaking up the vibes on any of Sydney's beautiful beaches

Create unforgettable memories as you ferry across the harbour and climb the iconic Sydney Harbour Bridge.

Day 3: Canberra

With its world-class museums (many of which have experiences just for kids) and a buzzy dining scene, Australia’s capital city of Canberra is an ideal getaway for culture-lovers of all ages.

The city’s compact size and proximity to nature make it easy for families to hop between award-winning cultural institutions and thrilling outdoor adventures on the same day.

Go hot air ballooning, cycle beautiful bike trails, or spend the day on Lake Burley Griffin

Day 4: Cairns

A family trip to Cairns is all about sharing the magic of one of the world’s greatest wonders – the Great Barrier Reef.

Start your adventure with a splash around in the Cairns Lagoon before heading out for a snorkel above the colourful coral

There's plenty to do between Cairns, Kuranda and Port Douglas, so having a flexible itinerary is ideal.

Spot crocodiles in the Daintree Rainforest, take a train to see waterfalls and ignite a true love of nature

Day 5: Gold Coast

Queensland’s Gold Coast is known as the ultimate family beach getaway. You’ll find a beguiling mix of things to do with the kids, from dining and culture to adventure and nature experiences – all alongside 57km (35mi) of white, sandy beaches

Visit Australia’s biggest selection of theme parks, meet native animals at a wildlife sanctuary and explore World Heritage-listed rainforests in the Gold Coast hinterland
