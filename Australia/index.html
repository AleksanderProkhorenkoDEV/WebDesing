Summary of our History

Australia, a vast continent and island country in the southern hemisphere, is home to a rich and unique history. Before the arrival of Europeans, indigenous Australian people occupied these lands for millennia, developing diverse and complex cultures that integrated closely with nature.

These Aboriginal groups maintained a deep connection to the land, expressed through their traditions, art and orally transmitted knowledge systems.

The 19th century saw significant change in Australia due to the gold rush, which attracted immigrants from around the world in search of their fortune. This period of population growth and economic development contributed to the formation of cities and the diversification of Australian society.

In 1901, Australia federated its colonies to form the Commonwealth of Australia, achieving political independence from the United Kingdom while maintaining cultural and legal ties.

The Best City Of Australia

Cairns

Cairns is a city located in the northeast of Australia. It is a very popular destination for foreign tourists due to its tropical climate and its proximity to numerous attractions.

The Great Barrier Reef is one of them and is less than an hour away by boa.

Our History:

The land on which Cairns stands originally belonged to the Yidinji Walubarra people, who still today recognize indigenous property rights in the area.

The territory on which the city was built is known in the Yidiny language as Gimuy.

The best place to visit

The Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the largest coral reef in existence, stretching for more than 2,300km.

Located between the northeast coast of the country and the Pacific Ocean, it belongs to the Australian state of Queensland. It is made up of more than 2,900 individual reefs, and more than 900 islands, atolls and coral cays

Daintree national park

Most of the national park is covered by humid jungle. The great Daintree forest has been preserved in the same way as it is seen today, for more than one hundred million years, which probably makes it the oldest rainforest on Earth.

Melbourne

Its multicultural spirit and the mix of architectural styles (Victorian and contemporary) are the most representative hallmark of the city of Melbourne, a place where local traditions merge with strong international influences

Our History:

Melbourne is the capital of the Australian state of Victoria and is the most populous city in Australia and Oceania.

It was the capital of Australia between 1901 and 1927, when it was moved to the planned city of Canberra.

The best place to visit

Grampians National Park

The Grampians National Park is a national park in Victoria, Australia, located 235 kilometers northwest of Melbourne.

The Grampians are notable for their series of sandstone hills

Street Art

The brilliant street art that adorns every corner of this sacred place is a reminder of a more strident past.

Perth

Perth is a city in Western Australia, capital of the state of Western Australia. It has 2,125,114 inhabitants, which makes it the fourth most populated city in Australia and the largest in the state, since almost three-quarters of the state's total population resides there.

Our History:

Perth, the capital of Western Australia, has a history dating back to British colonization in 1829. Founded by Captain James Stirling, the city experienced rapid growth during the gold rush in the 1890s.

The best place to visit

The Bell Tower

The Swan Bells are a set of 18 bells hanging in a purpose-built 82.5-metre-high (271 ft) copper and glass bell tower in Perth, Western Australia.

King Park and Botanic Garden

Kings Park is both one of the world's largest and most beautiful inner city parks and also a rich cultural heritage site.

It is home to the spectacular Western Australian Botanic Garden, which displays over 3,000 species of the State's unique flora - a sight unlike anywhere else in the world.

With cafes, bushland walk trails, immaculate gardens and a variety of children's play areas, leave plenty of time on your visit to discover the many sides of Kings Park.
